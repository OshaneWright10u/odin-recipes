Jamaican Curry Chicken

Description

Jamaican Curry Chicken is a staple dish in Caribbean cuisine, known for its robust flavors and aromatic spices. This dish combines tender chicken pieces simmered in a rich, spicy curry sauce infused with thyme, allspice, and Scotch bonnet pepper. The result is a deliciously warm and comforting meal that is perfect for any occasion. It’s traditionally served with white rice or rice and peas, making it a hearty and satisfying dish​

ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 3 tbsp Jamaican curry powder
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (finely chopped)
  • 2 potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 1 carrot (sliced)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions (chopped)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 tsp allspice

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, season the chicken pieces with salt, black pepper, and 2 tablespoons of curry powder. Allow it to marinate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight, to enhance the flavors​
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  3. Brown the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pot and brown it on all sides. This should take about 8-10 minutes. Be sure to stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Add Spices and Liquids: Stir in the remaining tablespoon of curry powder, allspice, and Scotch bonnet pepper. Add the chicken broth and coconut milk, stirring well to combine.
  5. Simmer: Add the potatoes, carrot, thyme, and green onions to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent burning
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with salt and black pepper as needed. If you prefer a thicker sauce, simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. Serve: Serve the Jamaican Curry Chicken hot over a bed of white rice or rice and peas. Garnish with additional thyme or green onions if desired.
