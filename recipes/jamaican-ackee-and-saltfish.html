Jamaican Ackee and Saltfish

Description

Jamaican Ackee and Saltfish is the national dish of Jamaica, celebrated for its rich flavors and unique combination of ingredients. This savory meal features ackee, a soft and buttery fruit, paired with salted codfish, creating a delightful balance of textures and tastes. It is typically served with traditional sides such as boiled green bananas, dumplings, or fried plantains, making it a versatile dish that is enjoyed at any time of the day

ingredients

  • ½ lb salted codfish
  • 1 dozen fresh ackees or 1 can of ackee (19 oz)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, finely chopped (optional)
  • ¼ cup cooking oil
  • Black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Saltfish: Soak the salted codfish in water overnight to reduce the saltiness. The next day, drain and boil it in fresh water for 5-7 minutes. Drain again, remove the bones and skin, and flake the fish into small pieces.
  2. Prepare the Ackee: If using fresh ackee, clean and remove the seeds. Boil the ackee until tender, then drain and set aside. If using canned ackee, drain and rinse under cold water
  3. Add the Saltfish: Stir in the flaked saltfish and cook for an additional 3 minutes. Add the diced tomato, thyme, and Scotch bonnet pepper (if using). Cook for another 2-3 minutes
  4. Add the Ackee: Gently fold the ackee into the mixture, being careful not to mash it. Cook for 3-5 minutes until the ackee is heated through. Season with black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with your choice of traditional Jamaican sides such as fried dumplings, boiled green bananas, or fried plantains.
