Jamaican jerk pork

Description

Jamaican-Style Jerk Pork is a classic Caribbean dish known for its bold, spicy flavors. This dish involves marinating pork in a fiery jerk sauce made with Scotch bonnet peppers, allspice, and a medley of other spices and herbs. The pork is then slowly grilled or baked to achieve a tender, juicy interior and a charred, smoky crust. It’s a perfect dish for those who love a bit of heat and a lot of flavor. Traditionally, it’s served with rice and peas, festival bread, or roasted breadfruit

Ingredient

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder or pork butt (cut into chunks)
  • 2 tbsp allspice berries (ground)
  • 1 tbsp thyme (dried or fresh)
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers (seeded and finely chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 4 green onions (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup white vinegar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a blender or food processor, combine the allspice, thyme, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, Scotch bonnet peppers, garlic, onion, green onions, brown sugar, soy sauce, white vinegar, vegetable oil, lime juice, salt, and black pepper. Blend until smooth to create the jerk marinade.
  2. Marinate the Pork: Place the pork chunks in a large resealable bag or a bowl. Pour the jerk marinade over the pork, ensuring all pieces are well coated. Seal the bag or cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld​ (That Girl Cooks Healthy)​​ (Jamaicans and Jamaica - Jamaicans.com)​.
  3. Grill the Pork: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the pork from the marinade and allow any excess to drip off. Grill the pork for about 10-15 minutes on each side, or until the pork is cooked through and has a nice char. You can also use a meat thermometer to check that the internal temperature has reached 145°F (63°C)​
  4. Bake Option: If you prefer baking, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the marinated pork on a baking sheet and bake for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C). Baste the pork occasionally with the leftover marinade for extra flavor.
  5. Serve: Once cooked, let the pork rest for a few minutes. Serve with traditional sides like rice and peas, festival bread, or roasted breadfruit.
